Q 3. Is there an easier way to take screenshots on my iPhone or iPad running iOS 10.2? A 3. Yes, there is a quicker way to take screenshots on iPhones or iPads running iOS 10.2. You simply need to enable the AssistiveTouch feature. Open the Settings app and go to General > Accessibility > AssistiveTouch. Enable the feature, and you’ll be able to take screenshots by tapping the AssistiveTouch button and then the Device option followed by the More option and then Screenshot.

Q 4. Are there any other ways to take screenshots on iOS 10.2? A 4. Yes, there are other ways to take screenshots on iOS 10.2. You can utilize the Siri voice command and say ‘Take a screenshot’, and then the screenshot will automatically be taken and saved in your Photos app. You can also connect your iPhone or iPad to your Mac or PC and use the Grab App on the Mac or Snipping Tool on Windows to capture screenshots.

Q 5. Can I capture a screenshot of the Lock Screen in iOS 10.2? A 5. Yes, you can capture a screenshot of the Lock screen in iOS 10.2.
