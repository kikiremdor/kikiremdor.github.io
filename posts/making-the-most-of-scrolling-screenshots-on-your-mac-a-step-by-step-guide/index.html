1. How to take a screenshot on Mac Using a Scrolling Screenshot#

Taking screenshots on a Mac has never been easier, thanks in part to the scrolling screenshot feature. This unique method of screenshotting enables users to capture entire pages, even if they’re not visible on the screen. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to use the feature on your Mac.

First, open the page you’d like to take a screenshot of. Then, press the combination of Shift+Command+5 on your keyboard. This will open the screenshot menu from where you can select the scrolling screenshot option.

Next, click the mouse and drag it down the page. This will prompt the Mac to take the screenshot. By scrolling down, you’ll capture the entire page in one go. When you’re done, simply release the mouse to finish the screenshot.

Finally, save the screenshot onto your desktop by clicking the save button. You can also open it up in an image tool and crop it or edit it as desired. You can then share the final image with your friends or colleagues.

With the scrolling screenshot feature, capturing entire pages on a Mac has never been easier. Follow the steps in this guide to make the most of it.

- Take a Scrolling Screenshot on Mac#

Taking a screenshot on a Mac is easy but often times it is limited to just capturing the visible area of a page. With a scrolling screenshot, it is possible to capture a longer or entire webpage. This helpful feature is available on Macs with the Mojave update or higher. Here is how to take a scrolling screenshot on a Mac.

First, use the shortcut Command-shift-3 to take a standard screenshot of the visible portion of a page. Then, open the Mac’s Preview app and select ‘Edit’ followed by ‘Take Screenshot’. Select the ‘From Entire Page’ option. The screenshot will capture the whole page, no matter how long it is.

If you need to capture only a specific part of the page, select ‘Selection’ in the Take Screenshot menu instead. This option lets you select a specific area of a page. The screenshot will capture the entire screenshot of the page and save it as a single image which can be edited later.

Finally, if you need to capture a scrolling screenshot of a long page, select ‘Timed Screen’ from the Take Screenshot menu. This will allow you to capture a scrolling screenshot of a page within a set time frame. This feature is great for capturing longer articles or specific content of a page.

The scrolling screenshot feature is a great way to capture entire pages on a Mac. With just a few clicks you can easily capture an entire page or just the specific portion you need. With the Mojave update or higher, anyone can take a scrolling screenshot on their Mac and save it to share with others.

- Edit and Share Your Screenshot#

Taking screenshots on Mac is incredibly useful for creating documents, sharing moments from your screen, and more. Ever since MacOS Mojave, you can take a scrolling screenshot — perfect for capturing a long webpage or chat. Here is how to take a screenshot on Mac using a scrolling screenshot.

First, open the app you want to take a scrolling screenshot of. Then, press Command + Shift + 5 keys all at the same time. This will bring up a screenshot toolbar at the bottom of your screen. Choose the Capture Entire Screen option.

Your mouse cursor will transform into a target and you can click and drag any area to capture. Once you have selected the box to begin the scrolling screenshot, click once more.

2. Simple Steps to Follow for Taking a Scrolling Screenshot in macOS#

Taking screenshots on macOS is easier than ever. A scrolling screenshot makes it even better. It allows you to capture an entire page in one image. This is a valuable tool for bloggers, students, and any other user of macOS. Here are a few simple steps to take a scrolling screenshot on macOS.

Firstly, make sure you have the latest version of macOS installed. This will ensure the best performance when taking the screenshot. Now, open the page you want to capture and press the Shift-Command-4 keys simultaneously. This will open the screenshot tool. You can drag the cursor over the area you want to capture.

When you have finished selecting the area, press the spacebar key. This will open a camera icon. Now hover the camera over the scroll bar on the side of the page. The entire page will be selected automatically. If you want to capture multiple pages, you can simply keep scrolling and clicking the spacebar.

Once you are done, press the Return/Enter key. This will take the screenshot. Now you can save the screenshot and edit it as you see fit. You can also share it directly to social media or other locations. Taking scrolling screenshots on macOS is now easy and convenient.

I. Taking a Scrolling Screenshot#

Taking scrolling screenshots on a Mac is an useful tool for capturing long lists or large article that don’t fit on your screen. Fortunately, the process is easy and can be done with a few keystrokes. Here are the simple steps to follow for taking a scrolling screenshot in macOS.

The first step is to press Command+Shift+3 simultaneously. This will capture the full screen with your mouse pointer included in the screenshot. You must make sure that you’ve selected the area correctly, as it will be difficult to go back and edit it afterward.

Next, open the screenshot in Preview mode. It should appear on your desktop as a .png file. After selecting the screenshot in Preview, click on the “View” tab followed by “Take a Screenshot”. This will give you the option to select the area you want to capture.

Once you’ve selected the desired area, click “Command+Shift+4” and drag the cross-hairs to the bottom left corner of the scrolling window. Then, drag the cross-hairs down until the desired area is selected.

Finally, press “Enter.” This will take your scrolling screenshot. You can then save it to your desktop or in a desired folder for future use. Taking a scrolling screenshot on a Mac is now a breeze with these simple steps!
