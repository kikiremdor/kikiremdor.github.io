1. Set up your phone after the factory reset#

After completing a factory reset on your Android phone, it is necessary to re-enter your Google account. If you don’t, you will not be able to access the Play Store and other Google services. This tutorial will show you how to remove your Google account from your phone without using a computer.

To start, go to Settings, then Accounts and select your Google account. Next, tap on Remove Account, and the account will be deleted. Make sure you back up any data before deleting your account, as any data associated with that account won’t be retrievable after removing it.

Now, it is time to create a new Google account and add it to your phone. From the Settings menu, select Accounts and then choose Add Account. Then, select Google, and enter your new or existing credentials. Once done, your Google account will be added and ready to use.

Finally, you can go back to the Google Play Store app and you should be able to access it with your new account. You should also be able to access all of your previous apps, music, movies, and books from your Google Play account. That’s it! Now your phone is all set up after a factory reset.

Second, you’ll need to go to Settings > Security > Device Administrators and select the Google Account you want to remove. Select Deactivate and the account will be removed from the device. Fourth, you’ll need to turn off the auto-sync feature for your Google Account. To do so, go to Settings > Accounts > Google > Auto-sync and disable the feature.
