Do you ever feel like you’re on a perpetual hamster wheel, having to manually transfer the same slew of contacts from your old iPhone to your new Android? It’s enough to make you want to concede defeat and stick with the same device for another few years – an endless trap, unfortunately.

But, fear not! You no longer have to suffer the hassle and bustle of manually transferring your contacts from one device to another. In today’s guide, you’ll learn an easy 7-step process to transfer your contacts from your iPhone to your Android using Gmail!

Using Gmail, it’s incredibly easy to transfer your contacts from your iPhone to your Android. All you need to do is download your contacts to a VCF file from your iPhone, open your Gmail contacts, and import your contacts from the file you downloaded. To make the process even easier, check out our comprehensive 7-step guide that’ll have you transferring your contacts in no time.

The first step is to open your iPhone’s Contacts app and click the “Groups” button in the upper-right corner. From there, you’ll select the “All on My iPhone” option which will enable you to select all your contacts to be downloaded to a VCF file. Once you’ve selected them all, click the blue “Done” button in the upper-right corner and you’ll be taken to the “Share Contact” page.

Next, you’ll click the “Share Contact” button in the top-right corner of the page, followed by the “Mail” link. You’ll be taken to a new window where you just enter your Gmail address in the “To” field and hit “Send”. You’ll then receive an email that contains the VCF file with all your contacts – this will be used in the next step!

The third step involves signing into your Gmail account and clicking on the “Contacts” link in the left sidebar. You’ll then be taken to your Gmail contacts page where you’ll click the “More” dropdown and select the “Import” option. You’ll be prompted to upload the VCF file you received in your email and click the “Import” button.

The fourth step is to open your Android device and sign in to your Gmail account. Then, open the Contacts app and select the “Settings” button in the bottom-right of the page. Under “External Accounts”, you’ll select the “Gmail” account. You’ll then be prompted to “Sync Contacts” and select the “Sync Now” button – your contacts will now be available on your Android device!

The fifth step is to open your Android Contacts app and select the “Menu” button in the top-right of the page. Select the “Merge Accounts” option and the “Gmail” account which will allow you to select the “Merge” button to combine your contacts.

The sixth step is to open the Contacts app again and click the “Menu” button in the top-right of the page. Select the “Display Options” option and verify your contacts are set to display in the “All Contacts” view. When you’re done, select the “Done” button in the top-right corner of the page.

And the last step is to open the Contacts app one final time and select the “Menu” button in the top-right of the page. This time, you’ll select the “Accounts” option which will allow you to select the “Gmail” account. After selecting this, you’ll be prompted to delete the account – make sure you select the “Sync Contacts” option to successfully complete the process, and you’re done!

The above 7-step guide outlines a simple process to transfer your contacts from your iPhone to your Android using Gmail. So, don’t waste any more time manually transferring your contacts from one device to another –with this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to easily transfer them in no time at all!
